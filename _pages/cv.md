<section class="pub-year">
  <h2>Research interests</h2>

  <article class="pub-card">
    <p>
      My research combines atomistic simulation, scattering experiments, and machine learning to understand structural disorder in framework materials. Current interests include:
    </p>

    <ul>
      <li>machine-learned interatomic potentials for flexible and disordered materials;</li>
      <li>total-scattering-constrained modelling of local structure;</li>
      <li>amorphous and glassy metal–organic frameworks;</li>
      <li>orientational disorder, strain coupling, and frustrated flexibility in MOFs;</li>
      <li>topological and geometric descriptors for crystalline and amorphous frameworks.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</section>

<section class="pub-year">
  <h2>Research experience</h2>

  <article class="pub-card">
    <ul>
      <li>Developed machine-learned interatomic potentials using GAP, ACE, and MACE frameworks.</li>
      <li>Generated DFT reference data using CP2K and VASP for modelling phase transitions in complex materials.</li>
      <li>Performed single-crystal total-scattering experiments for diffuse-scattering measurements at the ESRF.</li>
      <li>Developed refinement software for big-box modelling of amorphous materials using total-scattering data and force-field constraints.</li>
      <li>Built automated workflows for coarse-graining and back-mapping inorganic and hybrid framework structures.</li>
      <li>Synthesised and characterised metal–organic frameworks using hydrothermal synthesis, PXRD, and SCXRD.</li>
      <li>Synthesised and characterised metal–ammonia solutions using cryogenic techniques, neutron total scattering, SQUID magnetometry, and muon spin resonance.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>
</section>
